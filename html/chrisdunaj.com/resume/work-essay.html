I worked for Cigna as an intern in the summer of 2007, and returned as a full-time employee in June of 2008. Both the internship and my initial full-time employment were classified as an associate of the Technology Early Career Development Program (TECDP). The TECDP is a rotational program where associates receive specialized training and mentoring as they transition through roles in different teams within Cigna.

During my internship with Cigna, I stayed with other interns in an apartment community in Manchester, Connecticut, where we could commute together to work and socialize together outside of work. During my internship, I was on the Testing and Quality Control (TQC) team. The TQC lab imaged and built the physical hardware used for troubleshooting and testing of new software and updates for Cigna computers. In addition to helping with our standard duties on the team, I also learned a great deal about VMWare enterprise solutions as my team began the transition from a physical to a virtual test environment.

My first rotation in the full-time TECDP program was on the Web Administration Team. As a member of the team, I performed IIS changes and code move-ups in production and test environments. I worked in Windows and UNIX operating system environments to move, prepare, and run files, and often using IBM WebSphere and BEA WebLogic application server consoles. About halfway through the rotation, I was given the Service Lead role on the team, where I coordinated the monthly release activities between my team and other teams, managers, and stakeholders for the over 400 applications we supported.

My second rotation was on the Clarity Administration team. Clarity PPM is a project and portfolio management tool used throughout Cigna IT that provides visibility into current initiatives, budget and financial management, resource management, forecasting, and time tracking. In my role, I provided day-to-day technical and administrative support for Clarity's 5000+ users and stakeholders. My role required strong communication, problem solving, and customer service skills, as well as the fine attention to detail required when working with a production environment. I worked with my team to redesign our ticket intake site to allow us to properly track customer requests, and also created custom Microsoft SQL queries for accessing real-time ticket data and statistics for the team and management chain. Data from the ticket system indicated that during my time on the team, I handled 61% (over 1100) of our Clarity tickets.

My third and final rotation was in the Cigna Information Protection (CIP) organization as an Application Support Specialist. My duties in CIP were split between two applications: RSA Archer and Aveksa Access Management. Archer is an enterprise governance, risk management, and compliance (eGRC) solution. In my role supporting Archer, I provided administrative support and development for application owners and teams. I created one application from the ground up as a request form and management system to handle requests for assistance from subject matter experts. I also worked with other teams to make modifications and enhancements to their existing Archer applications as needed. Aveksa Access Management is an access governance system that uses a role-based access control (RBAC) structure that allows for granular control and easy management of accounts and entitlements. I developed email templates for system-generated notifications and training documents for pilot users. I assisted with project activities as we prepared to roll out the new system to the whole enterprise, and even helped the team with the initial wave of requests after I had already transitioned to my new role in another organization.

Before my graduation from the program, I was an active member of multiple TECDP committees. I began as a proofreader on the TECDP Proofreading team, under the Communications Committee. I later became the Proofreading Team Lead, where I managed incoming requests, assigned proofreaders, and notified stakeholders upon completion of their documents. I also was a member of the TECDP SharePoint team, under the Communications Committee, and of the Curriculum team, which was a project under the Development Committee.

Following my graduation from the Technology Early Career Development Program, I joined the Data Backup and Recovery team in the IT Operations organization. My day-to-day responsibilities on the team included configuration of IBM's Tivoli Storage Manager (TSM) on Windows and Linux client nodes, restoration of files and Exchange mailboxes, performing health checks and basic maintenance on TSM instance servers, and assisting with the team's large projects. At the time, our largest project was a migration from TSM to EMC's Avamar backup solution. In addition to moving clients from TSM to Avamar, I contributed to the Avamar migration by creating a tracking document used to manage client migrations and compile progress numbers and charts to be passed up the management chain. On the Data Backup and Recovery team, I also led the rollout of EMC's Data Protection Advisor (DPA) solution to monitor our team's backup jobs across TSM, Avamar, Oracle RMAN, and EMC DataDomain, and created scheduled and on-demand reports for senior management.

My current role is as a Web Hosting Engineer in the Web IT organization. My team provides 24/7 support to the infrastructure that runs our major web portals, including our customer, employer, and provider portals. We create and maintain an array of scripts to make management of our 300+ Linux, AIX, and Solaris servers easier. We use a variety of tools, including our homegrown scripts, to monitor our virtual machines and collect metrics on them. I serve as the team's go-to person for all tasks related to our central metrics, monitoring, and scripting server, and for all things related to our mobile apps, which also run on our infrastructure. Typically, whenever there is a question about our metrics/monitoring server one of the mobile servers, members of the team come to me for answers. Additionally, I have worked with members of our team and other teams to move the portals and applications we support to updated machines and software. Last year we moved some of our portal services from version 6.1 to version 8.5 of WebSphere Application Server, and this year we've been moving two of our portals to WebSphere Portal Server version 8.

Before joining Cigna, I was a SysAdmin Student for UConn's Student Affairs IT (SAIT) and an Advanced Computer Technician for ResNet (which became a member of SAIT in 2005). I began working at the beginning of my second semester, in January of 2005. I progressed through ResNet as an Entry Level Technician, a Call Center Technician, and eventually an Advanced Computer Technician, where I performed advanced troubleshooting on student computers and wrote training materials for our wiki, training manual, and semesterly training sessions. During my final year, I was given the opportunity to work as a SysAdmin Student, where I imaged and added new computers to our domain using Norton Ghost, and tested, installed, configured, and performed troubleshooting on software and hardware for all SAIT staff computers.
